Water is the foundation of all ecosystems on Earth. From vast oceans to small rivers and lakes, water provides habitats for countless species of plants and animals. The health of these aquatic environments is directly linked to the quality of the water they contain.

Oceans cover over 70% of the Earth's surface and are home to a massive diversity of marine life.

Coral reefs, often called the "rainforests of the sea," are critical to marine biodiversity.

Rivers and lakes provide essential freshwater resources for both wildlife and human communities.

Water is integral to land-based ecosystems, supporting forests and other terrestrial habitats.

Conservation efforts are vital to protect our water resources from pollution and overuse. By preserving water quality and managing its use responsibly, we can help ensure the long-term health of our planet's ecosystems and the species that depend on them.
